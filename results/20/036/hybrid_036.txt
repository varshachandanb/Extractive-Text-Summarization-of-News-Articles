Voters will have a clear choice between the politics of fear and the politics of hope in the next general election, said Charles Kennedy. On the Asian tsunami he said it had been "very heartening" to learn of the generosity being shown by Britons. At home he said many people were turning to the Liberal Democrats as they became disheartened with the politics of the other two main parties.The general election would be a three-party struggle, as the Conservative party "fades away" as a national force and the Liberal Democrats challenge Labour in its heartlands, he said. "A clear division is emerging in British politics - the politics of fear versus the politics of hope.
